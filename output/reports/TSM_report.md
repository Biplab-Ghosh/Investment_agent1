# Equity Research Note: Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company Limited (TSM)

## Executive Summary
Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company Limited (TSM) is a leading player in the semiconductor industry, boasting a wide economic moat primarily due to significant switching costs and a robust market position. Our discounted cash flow (DCF) analysis yields an intrinsic value of $2,433.62 per share, indicating a substantial upside of 501.9% from the current price of $404.35. Given the strong fundamentals and the margin of safety of 83.4%, we rate TSM as a "Buy."

## Valuation Analysis
Our DCF valuation is grounded in several key assumptions:
- **WACC**: We utilized a weighted average cost of capital (WACC) of 9.16%, reflecting the company's risk profile and capital structure.
- **Terminal Growth Rate**: A conservative terminal growth rate of 2.5% was applied, consistent with long-term GDP growth expectations.
- **Cash Flow Projections**: We forecasted TSM's free cash flows based on historical growth rates and market trends.

The intrinsic value of $2,433.62 per share suggests that TSM is significantly undervalued at its current trading price. The calculated margin of safety of 83.4% provides a cushion against potential market fluctuations and operational risks.

## Competitive Position
TSM's competitive position is characterized by a wide economic moat, primarily driven by high switching costs. Customers, particularly major technology firms, face significant hurdles in transitioning away from TSM due to the specialized nature of semiconductor manufacturing and the substantial investments already made in TSM’s technology. This strong moat supports our valuation, as it provides TSM with pricing power and customer loyalty, which are critical in the highly competitive semiconductor landscape.

## Key Risks
- **Geopolitical Tensions**: TSM operates in a region susceptible to geopolitical risks, particularly between the U.S. and China, which could disrupt operations or supply chains.
- **Technological Obsolescence**: Rapid advancements in technology could render existing manufacturing processes obsolete, necessitating continuous investment in R&D.
- **Market Volatility**: The semiconductor industry is cyclical, and economic downturns can lead to reduced demand for chips, impacting revenue and cash flows.
- **Competition**: Increasing competition from other semiconductor manufacturers, particularly in advanced nodes, could pressure margins and market share.

## Sensitivity Analysis
The intrinsic value range from our sensitivity analysis highlights the impact of varying assumptions on TSM's valuation:
- **Bear Case**: $1,485.71, assuming lower growth rates and higher competition.
- **Base Case**: $2,433.62, based on our primary assumptions.
- **Bull Case**: $4,066.98, reflecting optimistic growth scenarios and market expansion.
- **Monte Carlo P50**: $2,353.60, indicating a median outcome from a probabilistic analysis.

The primary drivers of this range include changes in revenue growth rates, operating margins, and the terminal growth rate. A more aggressive growth outlook in the bull case reflects TSM's potential to capture additional market share and innovate faster than competitors.

## Investment Conclusion
Based on our comprehensive analysis, we recommend a "Buy" rating for TSM, with a price target of $2,433.62 per share. This target reflects the company's strong competitive position, robust cash flow generation, and significant upside potential. The substantial margin of safety further enhances the attractiveness of this investment, making TSM a compelling opportunity in the semiconductor sector.
